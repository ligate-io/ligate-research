# PoUA v0.7.2 — Reviewer Reading Guide

A wayfinding doc for readers approaching the PoUA paper from a specific technical background. The paper itself is ~110 pages with appendices; this guide pre-routes you to the sections most likely to be load-bearing for your specialty.

If you're reading this without intent to review, the recommended starting point is the paper's §1.6.1 status panel (it categorises every claim as proven, bounded-under-assumptions, or empirical / heuristic) followed by the abstract and §1 in order.

## Load-bearing claims (where to push hardest)

These are the claims whose failure breaks PoUA. If any one falls, the paper needs revision.

### 1. Lemma 1 cost-to-grind floor (§5.5.3)

> $F_{\text{net}}(m, k) \geq \tau_{\text{burn}} \cdot \Delta r / (\eta \cdot \alpha_{\text{eff}}(m, k))$

The minimum non-recoverable fee an adversary must burn to lift their reputation by $\Delta r$ via grinding. This is the bound that makes the chain's security depend on $\tau_{\text{burn}}$ rather than on out-of-protocol scarcity.

**Where to push.** The proof structure relies on $\alpha_{\text{eff}}(m, k) = \alpha + (m-1)\beta/k$ — the "voter cartel coverage" correction. v0.6 of the paper had a different formula ($\alpha + m\beta/k$); the simulator caught the disagreement empirically, and v0.6.1 patched the proof. If you spot another disagreement between the proof formula and §4.3 of the paper, that is the highest-leverage critique.

**Validation.** Empirical figure at `prototypes/poua-sim/out/lemma1_burn_destinations.png`, generated by `prototypes/poua-sim/scripts/run_lemma1_scan.py`. Test vectors at `prototypes/poua-sim/test_vectors/lemma1_cost_to_grind.json`.

### 2. §6.2 Nash equilibrium claim

> "In PoUA at steady state, propose-all-valid + vote-honestly + don't-equivocate is a Nash equilibrium."

The argument is qualitative. We have not run rational agents that search the strategy space.

**Where to push.** This is the gap. §9.1 limit #2 acknowledges it. M6 of the simulator is designed to close this with empirical strategy-space search; design doc at `prototypes/poua-sim/docs/m6-design.md`. The five named deviations to verify are EQUIVOCATE, CENSOR_BY_SCHEMA, GRIND_VIA_SELF_ATTESTATION, GRIND_VIA_STAGED_SUBMITTERS, FREE_RIDE_VIA_VOTE_ONLY. If you can name a deviation we haven't covered, that's the most useful feedback.

**Validation.** Currently none. M6 work (3-4 weeks) pending. v0.8 paper will incorporate the M6 figures.

### 3. Transition-state $\kappa$ envelope (§5.3.1)

> $\kappa = \bar{r}_H / r_{\min}$ in the cold-start window where reputation has not yet equilibrated.

The chain's first ~30 days are the hardest period to defend; reputation has not had time to differentiate honest from adversarial validators.

**Where to push.** The envelope is empirical, not analytical. Figure at `prototypes/poua-sim/out/kappa_trajectory.png` shows realized $\kappa$ across the chain lifecycle. If your model of cold-start chain dynamics differs from ours, this is where the conversation gets interesting.

**Validation.** Empirical figure as above. Generator: `prototypes/poua-sim/scripts/run_kappa_trajectory.py`.

### 4. §5.5 layered defense (Layers 1-4)

> Compound capital-plus-grinding adversaries are bounded by the joint cost-to-grind across all four layers.

Layer 1 (canonical-address rejection of self-attestation), Layer 2 (proposer-submitter exclusion), Layer 3 (τ_burn enforcement on protocol fees), Layer 4 (statistical detectors §A.1 / §A.2 / §A.3).

**Where to push.** Layers 1 and 2 are address-graph based; staged-submitter adversaries (modeled in M6) try to evade them. We currently model the *successful evasion* (compound adversary uses configured "far" submitters); we don't model the *evasion attempt* (rational agent searching for staged configurations). If you have specific staging patterns we haven't covered, that's high-leverage feedback.

**Validation.** Figure at `prototypes/poua-sim/out/cost_to_attack.png`, generator at `prototypes/poua-sim/scripts/run_capital_scan.py`.

### 5. §A.4 detector FPR under realistic null

> The §A.3 bipartite-density detector's analytical FPR bound assumes Erdős-Rényi null hypothesis; reality is closer to scale-free.

We acknowledge this in §A.4 with an empirical figure showing the mismatch.

**Where to push.** Is the empirical mismatch we report consistent with what you'd expect from typical attestation-traffic graph topology? If the detector's TPR turns out to be close to its FPR (caught by M6), the §A.3 detector may need re-calibration before mainnet.

**Validation.** Figure at `prototypes/poua-sim/out/a3_fpr_comparison.png`, generator at `prototypes/poua-sim/scripts/run_a3_fpr_comparison.py`. TPR is unmeasured; M6 closes that gap.

## Known weaknesses we already see

We are not asking you to find these for us. We list them so you can prioritize critique on what we don't already know.

1. **§6.2 Nash claim is qualitative** (acknowledged in §9.1 limit #2). M6 closes this empirically.
2. **§A.3 detector TPR is unmeasured** (only FPR is empirically validated). M6 closes this.
3. **τ_burn calibration is for v0; production calibration requires devnet data**. The §4.4.2 adaptive rebase mechanism handles drift, but the initial calibration is a recommendation, not a measurement.
4. **Cold-start economic security is the worst case**. §5.3.1 transition-state $\kappa$ is meant to bound this; the bound is empirical, and we expect it to be tight rather than loose.
5. **Cross-schema interaction is single-schema only** in v0.7. Cross-schema composition is v2 territory ([paper #6](../cross-schema-composition/)).
6. **Hardware-wallet attacks are out of scope**. PoUA assumes validator master keys are secure off-chain; key-loss is a custodial problem, not a protocol problem.
7. **Quantum-resistant signatures are out of scope**. Signature scheme is plug-in; PoUA mechanism is signature-scheme-agnostic.
8. **Cross-chain attestation portability** is mentioned as future work but not specified. v0.7.2 covers Ligate Chain only.

## The shortest possible read

If you have 30 minutes total:

1. **§1.6.1 status panel** (2 minutes) — categorisation of every claim
2. **Abstract** (3 minutes) — thesis
3. **§5.5.3 Lemma 1 statement and proof** (10 minutes) — the load-bearing inequality
4. **§6.2 Nash equilibrium argument** (10 minutes) — the gap we acknowledge
5. **§9.1 limitations** (5 minutes) — what we know is not yet validated

Direction-of-attack feedback on any of those five is more valuable than line edits across the full paper.

If you have 2 hours: add §3 system model, §5.3 cost-to-attack, §A.4 detector FPR comparison, and the relevant section from your specialty in the table at the top of this guide.

If you have a full week: read the full paper plus the [reference simulator README](../../prototypes/poua-sim/README.md), and run `python scripts/check_citations.py` from the repo root to verify our claim-to-test discipline.
